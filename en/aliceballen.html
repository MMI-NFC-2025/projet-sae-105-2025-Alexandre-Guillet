The path of a pioneer

Born in Seattle, Alice Augusta Ball excelled in science from a very early age. She earned degrees in pharmacy and chemistry before moving to Hawaii for her master's research. It was there that she took on the greatest medical challenge of her time: the treatment of Hansen's disease (leprosy).

At only 23 years old, she develops what will be called the « Ball Method ». She succeeds in making the chaulmoogra oil injectable and soluble in blood, saving thousands of lives.

A posthumous recognition

Unfortunately, Alice dies prematurely at 24. After her death, the president of her university appropriates her research and gives his own name to the method. It will take decades for her name to be rehabilitated and for the Matilda effect that struck her to be denounced.
